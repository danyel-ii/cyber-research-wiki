# Red Teaming

## Overview
Red teaming is a goal-based adversary-emulation practice used to assess how well an organization detects, responds to, and withstands realistic attacks.

## Research angle
This page should become the hub for:
- definitions and scope
- differences from pentesting
- planning assumptions and success criteria
- mapping exercises to ATT&CK and detection engineering

## Distinguish from penetration testing
- Pentesting often focuses on identifying and validating exploitable weaknesses in scoped systems.
- Red teaming typically centers on realistic adversary behavior, chained objectives, and end-to-end defensive validation.
