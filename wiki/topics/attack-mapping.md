# Attack Mapping

## Overview
Attack mapping links observations, detections, procedures, or findings to an adversary-behavior framework such as MITRE ATT&CK.

## Why it matters here
Mapping helps normalize findings across sources and makes it easier to connect security testing to detection engineering, reporting, and comparative analysis.

## Typical uses
- connect source material to ATT&CK tactics and techniques
- compare findings across incidents, exercises, or tools
- identify coverage gaps in detections or testing plans
