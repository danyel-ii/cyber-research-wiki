# Lab Safety and Rules of Engagement

## Overview
This page covers practical boundaries for authorized work: lab isolation, scope, approvals, constraints, communication paths, and execution limits.

## Why it matters
Good research practice is not just technical. It also requires a safe environment and explicit authorization boundaries.

## Typical elements
- scoped targets and exclusions
- communication and escalation paths
- timing windows
- prohibited techniques
- evidence handling expectations
- rollback and incident procedures

## Environment choice considerations
Portable or pre-built assessment environments, including live images, virtual machines, cloud instances, and mobile adjuncts, can make authorized testing easier to stage quickly. They also raise practical ROE questions about storage persistence, default credentials, telemetry, and how evidence is preserved or destroyed after an engagement.
