# Scope

## Overview
This wiki covers cybersecurity research with an emphasis on authorized testing, security assessment, adversary emulation, threat-informed defense, and the interpretation of evidence from reputable sources.

## In scope
- security testing methodology
- penetration testing
- red teaming and adversary emulation
- web, cloud, network, endpoint, identity, and mobile security research
- ATT&CK-aligned behavior mapping
- defensive implications: detection, hardening, monitoring, mitigation
- legal, ethical, and rules-of-engagement questions for authorized work

## Out of scope by default
- unauthorized intrusion guidance
- exploit chains written as operator runbooks
- malware development instructions
- persistence or evasion tradecraft written for real-world misuse
- indiscriminate vulnerability hunting without an authorization context

## Inclusion test for new pages
A page belongs here if it helps answer one of these questions:
1. What is this concept or method?
2. How is it performed or evaluated in an authorized research context?
3. What evidence supports or challenges the current view?
4. What are the defensive implications?

## Open questions
- Should compliance and governance material live here or in a separate adjacent repo?
- Should vendor-product pages be first-class entities or only appear when they matter to a research question?
