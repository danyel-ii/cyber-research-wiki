# Evidence and Synthesis Path

## Overview
This path teaches a reader how to use the wiki as a research system: how to move from source material to synthesized knowledge, how to read disagreements, and how to extend the wiki without degrading it.

## Audience
- contributors who will ingest sources
- readers who want to judge evidence quality instead of only consuming summaries
- agents or humans using the wiki as a durable research layer

## Prerequisites
- [[paths/foundations]]

## Learning goals
- understand the difference between source pages, topic pages, entity pages, and analyses
- understand how contradictions and uncertainty are meant to be preserved
- understand how high-quality sources should reshape stable pages over time

## Reading sequence
1. [[taxonomy]] — review the evidence-quality and organizing axes
2. [[wiki-index]] — understand the current page families
3. [[sources/kali-linux-website-corpus-2026-04-05]] — inspect a source summary page closely

5. [[entities/kali-linux]] — see how source claims propagate into an entity page
6. [[topics/penetration-testing-distributions]] — see how source claims propagate into a topic page
7. [[analyses/certification-corpus-ingest-audit-2026-04-05]] — inspect a durable ingest audit and calibration of evidence quality
8. [[log]] — inspect how material changes are recorded over time
9. [[templates/source-summary-template]] — see the minimum structure expected of source pages
10. [[templates/topic-template]] — see how stable synthesis pages are expected to look
11. [[templates/analysis-template]] — see how reusable answers should be stored

## What to notice
- sources should expose provenance and extracted claims
- synthesis pages should compress, compare, and link, not merely repeat source prose
- uncertainty is part of the knowledge model and should remain visible
