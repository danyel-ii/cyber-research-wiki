# Methodology Core Path

## Overview
This path is for readers who already understand the wiki's purpose and now want a more structured view of authorized assessment methodology, execution constraints, and supporting environments.

## Audience
- learners moving from general orientation into assessment practice
- practitioners who want to compare adjacent methods cleanly
- contributors deciding where new methodology pages belong

## Prerequisites
- [[paths/foundations]]

## Learning goals
- distinguish assessment types by objective and scope
- understand where rules of engagement shape execution
- understand the role of assessment environments such as purpose-built distributions
- connect methodology pages to formal guidance

## Reading sequence
1. [[topics/penetration-testing]] — the main hub for active assessment work
2. [[topics/lab-safety-and-rules-of-engagement]] — execution boundaries and practical controls
3. [[topics/penetration-testing-distributions]] — assessment environments as enablers, not methodologies
4. [[entities/kali-linux]] — a concrete assessment-environment example
5. [[topics/red-teaming]] — where goals and realism expand beyond standard pentest scope
6. [[frameworks/nist-sp-800-115]] — the most relevant current framework anchor for assessment methodology
7. [[frameworks/owasp-wstg]] — web-focused testing guidance as a more specific framework example
8. [[sources/kali-linux-website-corpus-2026-04-05]] — review how a source becomes structured methodology-relevant knowledge in the wiki

## What to notice
- method pages should answer "why and when" before "with what tooling"
- environment pages should stay subordinate to authorization and ROE
- source pages are evidence inputs, not curriculum endpoints by themselves
