# Foundations Path

## Overview
This path is the best entry point for someone who wants to understand what this wiki is about, what kinds of cybersecurity work it covers, and how the major concepts fit together before diving into domain-specific material.

## Audience
- readers new to this wiki
- readers who know some security terms but want a more disciplined frame
- readers who need a clean distinction between authorization, methodology, and adversary behavior

## Prerequisites
- none

## Learning goals
- understand the wiki's scope and boundaries
- understand why authorization is a first-class organizing principle
- understand the difference between penetration testing, red teaming, and threat modeling
- understand why ATT&CK and formal frameworks matter in this repo

## What to notice
- authorization status is treated as more important than "hat" labels
- methodology pages should tell you what a practice is for, not just what tools are used
- framework pages exist to stabilize language and comparison across future sources
